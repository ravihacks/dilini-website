About

A food scientist connecting biochemistry, grains, and product innovation

With a strong background in food science, food chemistry, and biopolymer characterization, Dilini's work spans legume processing, plant proteins, starch, bioactive compounds, and analytical method development.

Her doctoral research investigated the hard-to-cook defect in Australian legumes, linking post-harvest storage to changes in starch, protein, lipid, and cell wall composition. She applies HPLC, GC, LC-MS/MS, UV-Vis, rheology, texture analysis, microstructure imaging, and microbial quality control to understand food quality and processing performance.

At The University of Queensland, Dilini contributes to premium food and beverage research through the Australian Food & Beverage Accelerator (FaBA), specialising in oat-based and plant-protein systems and translating laboratory insights into commercial, sustainable food solutions.

Research Focus

Areas of Scientific Inquiry

Grounded in analytical food science and applied to industry-facing food systems, Dilini's work connects composition, processing, quality, and sustainable product innovation.

Grain & Food Analysis

Advanced grain compositional analysis for premium food and beverage research, with attention to food quality, resource efficiency, and commercial relevance.

Legume & Biopolymer Chemistry

Investigation of starch, protein, lipid, cell wall, and polyphenol interactions in legumes, especially the hard-to-cook defect linked to post-harvest storage.

Food Chemistry & Processing

Analytical method development and process optimisation using chromatography, spectroscopy, rheology, texture analysis, and non-thermal technologies.

Quality & Product Stability

Quality control, microbial assessment, compositional analysis, and processing protocols that improve stability, texture, and manufacturing performance.

Sustainable Food Innovation

Development of plant-based and value-added food solutions that support sustainability, resource efficiency, and stronger links between research and industry.

Industry Collaboration

Interdisciplinary research partnerships across universities and food manufacturers, translating laboratory findings into practical food quality and product outcomes.
